End-of-Life Doula

I became an INELDA-trained End-of-Life Doula in December 2023.

I see my role as a doula in helping those who are dying and their loved ones to go through the dying process with dignity and with sass.

When I first began endurance efforts, I focused on doing them--and living my life--with sass.

This phrase means a lot to me, but at its core, its about:

  • Doing things on your terms
  • Doing things that feed your soul and feel right to you--regardless of what society at large tells you you "should" do or want
  • Living life as fully and as present as possible

In dying and death work, these same principles apply.

In the United States, we've treated death as a medical event for far too long, when it's actually one of the two most natural, organic processes in our lives (the other being birth). If you're looking for something in addition to medical care or hospice help as you are near the end of life, a doula like me may be a good fit.

Services

As a doula, I can help with:

  • Providing resources to help dying people and their loved ones process and grieve
  • Helping to set up advanced care directives
  • Listening and creating space for you to process what's going on, what your wishes are, how you're feeling, and more. A lot comes up for us when the end is near, and we honor ourselves and each other by creating and holding the space to share whatever comes up.
  • Helping with plans for the death, funeral arrangements, and so forth
  • Remembrance or legacy projects to help a dying person and/or their loved ones find meaning in their lives
  • Guided imagery or meditation to help manage pain and anxiety
  • Determining appropriate rituals or practices for visiting/attending the dying person
  • Helping dying people and their loved ones understand what the process looks like
  • Serving as a non-clinical presence and advocate for the dying person
  • Making sure a dying person's rituals or wishes are identified and communicated--whether that's what they want to happen before death or after it
  • Planning/discussion of what a dying person and their loved ones want to happen when death is imminent, when death occurs, and afterwards
  • Ensuring that those plans/discussion actually happen when the time comes
  • After death, one or two reprocessing sessions to help loved ones begin to process the experience. I'm not a grief counselor, but these sessions serve as a good starting place and I can refer you to trained grief counselors in our area.

In short, I can help provide resources and set expectations to remove a lot of the logistics and uncertainty from the process, while also being a dedicated advocate and listening ear as you transition out of this life.

What I am not:

  • A trained clinical professional, such as a nurse, aide, or doctor. I don't provide direct medical care.
  • A therapist or grief counselor
  • A substitute for medical care, hospice care, hospice facilities, etc.

The process

I generally try to offer my services as a package, so we can discuss what your needs are, what you can afford, and come up with a service plan that works for you.

Where I work

I currently live in Harpswell, Maine, and in-person I serve communities within a 45-minute drive of my home, including Brunswick, Topsham, West Bath, Bath, Woolwich, Phippsburg, Durham, Freeport, Gardiner, and Augusta. If you're not sure if I cover your area, just ask!

I'm also available for virtual sessions to a much wider area, if you're comfortable with those sessions and not having me physically present.
